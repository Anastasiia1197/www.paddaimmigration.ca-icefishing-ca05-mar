Paddai Immigration Privacy Policy – Transparency and Protection of Your Data

Last updated: 30 March 2026

At Paddai Immigration, we place great importance on protecting your personal data. This privacy policy explains how we collect, use, share, and protect your data when you use our immigration advisory services and information portal in Canada 🇨🇦. We are fully transparent about our data processing practices and operate in accordance with stringent Canadian privacy regulations, including the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA) and applicable provincial privacy laws.

What data we collect and how it improves your experience

To provide you with the best immigration advisory and user experience on our platform, we collect specific personal data. This data helps us improve our content and services, and provide you with a safe and personalized experience tailored to your Canadian immigration journey.

Personal data enabling identification

  • Full name, email address, and phone number (when you create an account, sign up for newsletters, or request a consultation).
  • Contact details and background information when submitting inquiries via our contact form or assessment request.
  • Immigration-related information such as nationality, country of residence, education, work experience, and language proficiency (to assess eligibility).
  • Account data enabling access to personalized content and case management portal.

Technical and usage data

  • IP address and device information for security and fraud prevention purposes.
  • Browser type and version to optimally display and ensure the performance of our site.
  • Page views, click behaviour, and visited content to analyse interests and improve our services.
  • Interaction data with our website, tools, and client portal.

All our data collection activities comply with Canadian 🇨🇦 privacy regulations (PIPEDA) and aim to improve your experience on the Paddai Immigration platform. We never collect more data than is strictly necessary for the services provided.

The role of cookies in personalising your experience

Cookies are small text files stored on your device to optimise your experience on Paddai Immigration. They play a key role in providing a secure and personalised browsing environment for our visitors across Canada.

Types of cookies we use

  • Essential cookies: Necessary for basic functions, security, and integrity of the website.
  • Functional cookies: Store your preferences (language, region) and settings.
  • Analytical cookies: Help us understand how visitors use our site so we can improve our content and services.
  • Marketing cookies: Used to measure the relevance of informational communication (always with consent).

Managing cookies

You retain full control over cookie settings via your browser or our cookie banner. Please note: disabling essential cookies may affect the functionality of parts of the Paddai Immigration website. Our cookie settings are specifically tailored to the needs of users in Canada 🇨🇦 and comply with Canadian privacy regulations.

How we responsibly use your data to improve our services

Your data is used only for legitimate purposes that improve your user experience on the Paddai Immigration platform and guarantee security and professional immigration advisory services.

Rigorous data sharing protocols with trusted partners

Paddai Immigration shares your data only with carefully selected partners when necessary to provide our services (e.g., hosting, secure document storage, or analytics). All partners must meet our rigorous privacy and security standards and are contractually bound as "processors" under applicable Canadian law.

Categories of data recipients

  • Hosting and IT service providers: For the technical operation and security of our website and client portal.
  • Analytics services (e.g., Google Analytics): For anonymous analysis of site usage to optimise our content.
  • Email service providers: For sending service or informational emails (if you have subscribed).
  • Legal and regulatory authorities: Only when there is a clear legal obligation under Canadian law or by court order.
  • Secure document storage partners: For encrypted storage of client immigration files and supporting documents.

International data transfers

If data is transferred to countries outside Canada, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place, such as contractual clauses that maintain PIPEDA-equivalent protection. Our data processing always remains compliant with Canadian data protection law, regardless of where the data is processed.

Your rights to access, correct, or delete your data

Under Canadian privacy law (PIPEDA) and applicable provincial legislation, you have extensive rights regarding your personal data. Paddai Immigration fully respects these rights and provides simple procedures to exercise them.

Your privacy rights

  • Right of access: Insight into what data about you is being processed.
  • Right to rectification: Correcting inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure: Deletion of your data under certain conditions, subject to regulatory retention requirements.
  • Right to withdraw consent: Withdraw consent for processing where consent is the legal basis.
  • Right to complain: File a complaint with the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada (OPC) if you believe your rights have been violated.

How to exercise your rights

  1. If you have a client account: log in and update your information in the client portal.
  2. Submit a request via our online contact form.
  3. Contact our Privacy Officer directly using the email address below.
  4. Clearly specify which right the request concerns and provide identification (for verification).
  5. We will respond to your request within the statutory period of 30 days (or as required by PIPEDA).

For questions regarding your privacy rights, you can contact our Privacy Officer at privacy@paddaimmigration.ca. All requests are handled in accordance with Canadian data protection law.

Keeping you informed – updates to our privacy policy

We regularly review our privacy policy and may make changes to adapt to new legal requirements or improvements to our services. We assure you that you will be informed of significant changes in a timely manner.

Notification of changes

  • The 'Last updated' date at the top of this policy will be changed.
  • Important changes will be communicated via a banner on our website or email (for registered clients).
  • The policy will always be available at the same link.

Previous versions

Previous versions of our privacy policy will remain available upon request. We recommend periodically reviewing this policy. By continuing to use the Paddai Immigration platform after changes are made, you accept the updated policy.

For questions regarding this privacy policy or our data processing, you can contact our Privacy Team at privacy@paddaimmigration.ca or by mail: Paddai Immigration, Attn: Privacy Officer, 123 Immigration Avenue, Suite 400, Toronto, Ontario M5V 1A1, Canada 🇨🇦.
